<p class="ed-eyebrow">§ 01 · Frame</p>
<h2 class="ed-h">Process, environment <em>and listening</em>.</h2>
<p class="ed-lede">
ADAR offers artistic residencies to composers, performers and visual artists at the Leiguarda base (Belmonte de Miranda). Stays designed so that the work finds its form without the usual calendar pressure.
</p>
</section>

<section class="ed-objectives">
<p class="ed-eyebrow">§ 02 · How they work</p>
<h2 class="ed-h">Four operating principles.</h2>
<ol class="ed-list">
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">01</span><div><strong>Process, not product.</strong> The residency measures success by the path, not the outcome. Silence, doubt and rewriting are welcome.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">02</span><div><strong>Territory and heritage.</strong> Landscape, architecture and local history enter the work. The piece does not "use" the place: it converses with it.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">03</span><div><strong>Mediation and community.</strong> Open rehearsals, encounters with locals, talks. The residency is also conversation.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">04</span><div><strong>Archive and continuity.</strong> Each residency is documented with recording, photography and video, and published as an open archive piece.</div></li>
</ol>
</section>
