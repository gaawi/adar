<p class="ed-eyebrow">§ 01 · Leiguarda 47</p>
<h2 class="ed-h">Where ADAR <em>lives and works</em>.</h2>
<p class="ed-lede">
Living, working and presenting under one roof. An old barn in Leiguarda, Belmonte de Miranda, restored as a rehearsal hall, production studio and lodging for international residencies. The headquarters of Festival ADAR and the daily anchor of the association.
</p>
</section>

<section class="ed-objectives">
<p class="ed-eyebrow">§ 02 · What the base offers</p>
<h2 class="ed-h">Four functions, one building.</h2>
<ol class="ed-list">
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">01</span><div><strong>Rehearsal hall and concerts.</strong> Acoustically prepared space with a grand piano, equipped for long rehearsals, recording and micro-concerts for an intimate audience.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">02</span><div><strong>Production studio.</strong> Equipment for audio recording, video editing and audiovisual documentation of every residency and festival action.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">03</span><div><strong>Lodging.</strong> Rooms and common areas for artists in residence, with kitchen, full bathroom and shared workspaces.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">04</span><div><strong>Setting.</strong> A village of around 100 people, surrounded by meadows, granaries and trails. Silence, landscape and focus.</div></li>
</ol>
</section>

<section class="ed-mode">
<p class="ed-eyebrow">§ 03 · How we work here</p>
<h2 class="ed-h">Concerts, open rehearsals <em>and encounters</em>.</h2>
<div class="ed-twocol">
<div>
<h3>Creative residencies.</h3>
<p>
Stays from one to several weeks for composers, performers and visual artists. The pace is set by the work, not the calendar: silence, landscape and full attention.
</p>
<h3>Documentation and production.</h3>
<p>
Every residency is accompanied by careful audiovisual recording: audio, photography and video that extend the life of the work beyond the concert.
</p>
</div>
<div>
<h3>Mentorship.</h3>
<p>
Curatorial support from the co-direction and visiting specialists: score reading, staging analysis, historical contextualisation.
</p>
<h3>Local connection.</h3>
<p>
Every residency includes encounters with the people of Belmonte de Miranda: open rehearsals, talks, dinners and, where relevant, workshops with the rural primary school.
</p>
</div>
</div>
</section>
