<section class="ai-section ai-light" aria-label="Introduction">
<div class="ai-wrap">
<div class="ai-head">
<p class="ai-eyebrow">The Place</p>
<h2>Where ADAR lives and works</h2>
<p class="ai-sub">
Leiguarda 47 is the permanent home of Festival ADAR. A restored village house in the mountains of western Asturias where artists come to rehearse, compose, record, collaborate, and share their work with a local audience. Everything happens under one roof: sleeping, cooking, practicing, thinking, performing.
</p>
</div>
</div>
</section>

<!-- THE HOUSE -->
<section class="ai-section ai-cream" id="the-house" aria-label="The House">
<div class="ai-wrap">
<div class="ai-head">
<p class="ai-eyebrow">The House</p>
<h2>One building, many lives</h2>
<p class="ai-sub">
Not a concert hall. Not a hotel. Leiguarda 47 is a working house where artistic creation and daily life share the same space. Residents live together, eat together, and build projects side by side.
</p>
</div>

<!-- SPACES FOR CREATION -->
<section class="ai-section ai-light" id="spaces" aria-label="Spaces for creation">
<div class="ai-wrap">
<div class="ai-head">
<p class="ai-eyebrow">Spaces</p>
<h2>Built for focused, flexible work</h2>
</div>

<div class="ai-grid-2">

<article class="ai-card">
<h3>Rehearsal and Concert Room</h3>
<p>
The main room holds a grand piano and works as both rehearsal space and intimate performance venue. Chamber music, interdisciplinary rehearsals, readings, and small concerts all happen here.
</p>
</article>

<article class="ai-card">
<h3>Studio and Work Areas</h3>
<p>
Dedicated zones for composing, editing, digital production, and project planning. The house is equipped with projectors, creative software, audio capture tools, and high-speed fiber internet.
</p>
</article>

<article class="ai-card">
<h3>Living Quarters</h3>
<p>
Bedrooms, a shared kitchen, common areas, and terraces. Living on site means the conversation never stops and collaboration happens naturally, between sessions, over meals, on evening walks.
</p>
</article>

<article class="ai-card">
<h3>Outdoor Spaces</h3>
<p>
Terraces, village paths, and the surrounding mountain landscape. The outside is not separate from the work. Walking, listening, and simply being in this place shape the creative process.
</p>
</article>

</div>
</div>
</section>

<!-- LIVING AND WORKING TOGETHER -->
<section class="ai-section ai-cream" aria-label="Residency life">
<div class="ai-wrap">
<div class="ai-head">
<p class="ai-eyebrow">Residency Life</p>
<h2>A shared rhythm</h2>
<p class="ai-sub">
At Leiguarda 47, artists live and create in close proximity. The structure is simple: mornings for focused work, afternoons for rehearsal and exchange, evenings for reflection and informal conversation. Mentors, collaborators, and the ADAR team are all part of the daily fabric.
</p>
</div>

<section class="ai-section ai-light" aria-label="Concerts and gatherings">
<div class="ai-wrap">
<div class="ai-head">
<p class="ai-eyebrow">Public Moments</p>
<h2>Small concerts, close listening</h2>
<p class="ai-sub">
The headquarters hosts intimate performances, open rehearsals, work-in-progress sharings, and talks. The scale is deliberate: a few dozen seats, direct contact between artists and audience, music heard up close. Projects developed in residence find their first public life right here.

<div class="ai-wrap">
<div class="ai-head">
<p class="ai-eyebrow">The Territory</p>
<h2>Silence, landscape, scale</h2>
<p class="ai-sub">
Leiguarda is a village of fewer than twenty inhabitants in the mountains of Belmonte de Miranda. The quiet, the slow pace, and the distance from urban life are not incidental. They are the conditions that make a different kind of artistic work possible.
</p>

<!-- WHAT HAPPENS HERE -->
<section class="ai-section ai-light" aria-label="What happens here">
<div class="ai-wrap">
<div class="ai-head">
<p class="ai-eyebrow">What Happens Here</p>
<h2>From first ideas to finished work</h2>
</div>

<div class="ai-grid-2">

<article class="ai-card">
<h3>Residencies</h3>
<p>
Extended stays for composers, performers, and interdisciplinary artists. Time to develop new work, test ideas, rehearse intensively, and refine projects before they travel further.
</p>
</article>

<article class="ai-card">
<h3>Documentation and Production</h3>
<p>
Video, photography, audio recording, and digital production. Work made at Leiguarda 47 is captured and prepared to circulate beyond the residency itself.
</p>
</article>

<article class="ai-card">
<h3>Mentorship and Exchange</h3>
<p>
One-on-one feedback, collective sessions, and informal conversation between artists, mentors, and the ADAR team. The house is built for dialogue.
</p>
</article>

<article class="ai-card">
<h3>Community Connection</h3>
<p>
Open sessions, local encounters, and connections with nearby villages and heritage sites. The headquarters is a starting point for cultural work that reaches into the wider territory.
</p>
</article>

</div>
</div>
</section>

<!-- FACILITIES -->
<section class="ai-section ai-cream" aria-label="Practical information">
<div class="ai-wrap">
<div class="ai-head">
<p class="ai-eyebrow">Facilities</p>
<h2>What the house provides</h2>
</div>

<div class="ai-grid-2">

<article class="ai-card">
<h3>Equipment</h3>
<p>
Grand piano, projectors, monitors, creative software, audio and video recording tools, high-speed fiber internet for remote collaboration and streaming.
</p>
</article>

<article class="ai-card">
<h3>Accommodation</h3>
<p>
On-site bedrooms, shared kitchen, and common living areas. All meals and daily life are integrated into the residency experience.
</p>
</article>

<article class="ai-card">
<h3>Accessibility</h3>
<p>
Key spaces can be organized at ground level, including the rehearsal room, kitchen, and bathroom. ADAR plans individually with each resident to address access needs in advance.
</p>
</article>

<article class="ai-card">
<h3>Sustainability</h3>
<p>
Concentrating everything in one place reduces transport, simplifies logistics, and keeps the environmental footprint low. Local sourcing and reuse of existing resources are part of how the house operates.
</p>
</article>

</div>
</div>
</section>
