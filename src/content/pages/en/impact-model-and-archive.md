<p class="ed-eyebrow">§ 01 · Impact</p>
<h2 class="ed-h">Five editions, a <em>replicable model</em>.</h2>
<p class="ed-lede">
ADAR has produced five consecutive editions between 2021 and 2025, consolidating a stable programme in municipalities affected by depopulation. The project grew from a budget of €8,000 in 2021 to a projected €148,000 in 2026, building a professional cultural structure in rural Asturias.
</p>
</section>

<section class="ed-objectives">
<p class="ed-eyebrow">§ 02 · Model</p>
<h2 class="ed-h">Six operating pillars.</h2>
<ol class="ed-list">
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">01</span><div><strong>Stable annual programme.</strong> A long season with continuous residency and itinerant festival in August — not isolated events.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">02</span><div><strong>Physical base.</strong> Leiguarda 47: rehearsal hall, production studio and lodging. Continuity sustained by a concrete place.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">03</span><div><strong>Parity co-direction.</strong> Guillermo Laporta and Josefina Urraca. Shared artistic and production decisions.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">04</span><div><strong>Commissioning creators.</strong> We produce new work: 20+ world premieres in five editions.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">05</span><div><strong>Close mediation.</strong> Open rehearsals, sound walks, workshops with the rural primary school, intergenerational activities.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">06</span><div><strong>Audiovisual documentation.</strong> Every action carefully recorded for press, archive and onward circulation.</div></li>
</ol>
</section>

<section class="ed-mode">
<p class="ed-eyebrow">§ 03 · Archive</p>
<h2 class="ed-h">Living memory, <em>not museum</em>.</h2>
<div class="ed-twocol">
<div>
<p>
Each edition is documented and published as an open digital archive. 49 concerts, 15 site-specific installations, 12 sound walks and 8 artistic residencies already form the body of the project.
</p>
<p>
The archive is used to sustain applications to new calls, to accompany programmers on exploratory visits, and to feed critical conversation about living arts in rural areas.
</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>
The <a href="/en/category/projects-archive/">projects archive</a> gathers every piece with technical specs, photography, video and — when available — score or audio recording.
</p>
<p>
The <a href="/en/prensa/">press wall</a> collects every media mention since the first edition. An open, browsable archive, unfiltered.
</p>
</div>
</div>
</section>
