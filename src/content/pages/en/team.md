<p class="ed-eyebrow">§ 01 · Direction</p>
<h2 class="ed-h">People at the helm <em>of the project</em>.</h2>
<p class="ed-lede">
Complementary careers in creation, performance, production and site-specific experience design. A parity co-direction between Asturias and New York.
</p>
</section>

<section class="ed-twocol">
<div>
<p class="ed-kicker mono">Executive Director</p>
<h3>Guillermo Laporta.</h3>
<p>
<strong>Guillermo Laporta</strong> is a flutist, composer and designer based between Asturias and New York. His work has been featured in The New York Times, The New Yorker, Time Out, The Huffington Post, The Washington Post, EL PAÍS, El Mundo and RTVE.
</p>
<p>
His compositions include the opera-film <em>Architecture of a Common Man</em> (2023), the ballet-opera <em>Two Roads</em> (2020), the opera <em>Noctum</em> (2011) and the musical <em>LONDON, The Show</em> (2009).
</p>
<p>
Founder and executive director of CreArtBox (NYC) and Festival ADAR (Asturias). As a performer, he has collaborated with the BBC Orchestra and appeared at Carnegie Hall and Lincoln Center. Trained at the Royal College of Music (2008–2012).
</p>
</div>
<div>
<p class="ed-kicker mono">Artistic Director</p>
<h3>Josefina Urraca.</h3>
<p>
<strong>Josefina Urraca</strong> is a Spanish pianist and co-director of CreArtBox and Festival ADAR. Praised by <em>Mundo Clásico</em> for performances marked by "introspection and musical abandon", her art combines expressive depth, refined sound and poetic imagination.
</p>
<p>
She has performed at Salle Cortot in Paris, the Sony Auditorium in Madrid, Carnegie Hall in New York and Monten Hall in Tokyo. Her work goes beyond performance, including artistic concept, programming and collaboration with composers and visual artists.
</p>
<p>
Trained with Maria João Pires, Dmitri Bashkirov, Ferenc Rados and Josep Colom in Madrid, Paris, London and New York. Born in Palencia, she divides her artistic life between New York and Asturias.
</p>
</div>
</section>
