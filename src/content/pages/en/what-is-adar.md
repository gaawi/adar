<p class="ed-eyebrow">§ 01 · Definition</p>
<h2 class="ed-h">
A travelling cultural platform <em>rooted</em> in a hundred-soul village.
</h2>
<p class="ed-lede">
ADAR is the <strong>Association for the Development of the Arts in Rural Areas</strong>, a non-profit cultural initiative founded in 2020 in Leiguarda (Belmonte de Miranda, Asturias) by musicians Guillermo Laporta and Josefina Urraca. The festival born from the association produces classical music, contemporary visual art and activated heritage across villages, churches, granaries and landscapes of rural Asturias.
</p>
</section>

<section class="ed-twocol">
<div>
<p class="ed-kicker mono">Origin of the name</p>
<h3>From acronym to programme.</h3>
<p>
Festival ADAR takes its name from the association that organises it and articulates its cultural vision. The acronym became programme: <em>Arts / Development / Rural</em>.
</p>
</div>
<div>
<p class="ed-kicker mono">The association</p>
<h3>Two musicians, one village.</h3>
<p>
Guillermo Laporta (flute) and Josefina Urraca (piano) founded the association during the pandemic to activate rural Asturian heritage through music and living arts, supported by new technologies and an international network.
</p>
</div>
</section>

<section class="ed-objectives">
<p class="ed-eyebrow">§ 02 · Objectives</p>
<h2 class="ed-h">Six lines that cross every edition.</h2>
<ol class="ed-list">
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">01</span><div><strong>Revitalisation.</strong> International-impact cultural projects in rural Asturian areas with little specialised cultural programming.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">02</span><div><strong>Outreach.</strong> Bringing music and the arts to depopulating territories with intimate formats and close mediation.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">03</span><div><strong>Activated heritage.</strong> Re-using unconventional or unused rural spaces: granaries, churches, monasteries, palaces, chapels, markets.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">04</span><div><strong>Cultural tourism.</strong> Encouraging rural tourism through culture and attracting international artists to the territory.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">05</span><div><strong>Global connection.</strong> Linking Asturian rural areas with international audiences and circuits — New York, Porto, Paris, Berlin.</div></li>
<li><span class="ed-list-n mono">06</span><div><strong>Art with purpose.</strong> Using the arts to address contemporary social challenges: depopulation, identity, sustainability, just transition.</div></li>
</ol>
</section>

<section class="ed-mode">
<p class="ed-eyebrow">§ 03 · How it works</p>
<h2 class="ed-h">Residency and festival <em>as one ecosystem</em>.</h2>
<div class="ed-twocol">
<div>
<p>
ADAR unfolds in Asturian villages, towns and parishes of fewer than 500 inhabitants. The <strong>residency</strong> and the <strong>festival</strong> work as a single creative ecosystem: rehearsal, creation, mediation and concert happen in the same territory, with the same artists and the same host community.
</p>
<p>
The residency runs year-round at the Leiguarda base, where the work that later premieres at the itinerant August festival is prepared, touring between five and ten municipalities each edition.
</p>
</div>
<div>
<p>
The programme combines <strong>chamber music</strong>, <strong>site-specific installations</strong>, <strong>sound walks</strong>, <strong>video-mapping</strong>, <strong>dance</strong> and <strong>commissions of new work</strong> from contemporary composers.
</p>
<p>
All events are free admission until capacity, sustained with public support, private patronage and the complicity of the host councils.
</p>
</div>
</div>
</section>
