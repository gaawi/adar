<h1 class="adar-archive-title">Concert at the Palacio de Miranda-Valdecarzana (Grado, 2022)</h1>

<p class="adar-archive-lede">
The Palacio de Miranda-Valdecarzana, one of the most remarkable venues to have hosted the ADAR Festival musicians, opened its doors for a chamber music evening that sold out all available seats. It was the first of many nights the Festival would celebrate in this emblematic palace in Grado.
</p>

<div class="adar-archive-body">
<p>
The Palacio de Miranda-Valdecarzana, an architectural gem of Grado, revealed itself as one of the most beautiful and evocative spaces ever to host ADAR Festival concerts. The venue filled quickly, and when the main hall could hold no more listeners, the audience moved upstairs, leaning over the balcony to attentively follow every piece performed.
</p>

<p>
That was the first time the ADAR Festival visited this palace, a space that would become a recurring venue in subsequent editions. The acoustics of the main hall, the proximity between musicians and audience, and the beauty of the surroundings created an intimate, unrepeatable experience.
</p>

<h3 style="margin-top:28px">Programme</h3>

<p>
<strong>Johannes Brahms</strong> – Quinteto para clarinete y cuerdas en si menor, op. 115<br>
<strong>Antonín Dvořák</strong> – Cuarteto americano en Fa Mayor, op. 96<br>
<strong>Caroline Shaw</strong> – Blueprint<br>
<strong>Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart</strong> – Quinteto para clarinete y cuerdas en La Mayor, KV 581<br>
<strong>JP Jofre</strong> – Como el Agua y Primavera
</p>
