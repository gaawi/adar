<h1 class="press-clipping-title">Sound walks and concerts in Grado, Salas, Somiedo and Belmonte</h1>

<p class="press-lede">
From 8 to 18 August 2024, the ADAR Festival holds its fourth edition, offering a cultural experience that blends the beauty of rural Asturias with contemporary artistic expression.
</p>

<p class="press-byline">Editorial staff / Grado</p>

<p>
Organised by the Association for the Development of the Arts in Rural Areas, the festival features musicians and artists with international careers. It is structured around two main strands: the <strong>Artist Residency</strong> in Leiguarda (Belmonte de Miranda) and the <strong>Festival ADAR en Ruta</strong>, a travelling component that brings classical and contemporary music to various rural communities across Asturias.
</p>

<p>
The programme brings together Jone de la Fuente (violin), Almudena Rivas (viola), Natania Hoffman (cello), Mario Molina (piano), Guillermo Laporta (flute) and Josefina Urraca (piano), with a repertoire that includes works by Mozart, Debussy, Vivaldi and contemporary composers.
</p>

<p>
The highlights of the 2024 route:
</p>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Thursday 8 — Grado.</strong> Opening with art installations.</li>
  <li><strong>Tuesday 13 — Salas.</strong> Concert at the Monastery of San Salvador (20:00).</li>
  <li><strong>Wednesday 14 — Pola de Somiedo.</strong> Concert at the Market (20:00).</li>
  <li><strong>Sunday 18 — Leiguarda.</strong> Open-air closing concert with an art installation, the participation of the dancers Rebeca Martín Tassis and Izar Gayo, and a get-together with cider and empanada (21:30).</li>
</ul>
