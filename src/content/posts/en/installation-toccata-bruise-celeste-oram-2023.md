<h1 class="adar-archive-title">Installation: Toccata and Bruise (Celeste Oram)</h1>

<p class="adar-archive-lede">
Composer and projection designer Celeste Oram, originally from New Zealand and based in New York, lent her piece <em>Toccata and Bruise</em> for the 2023 edition of the ADAR Festival. An audiovisual installation that explores the musical concept of touch, presented at ADAR's headquarters in Leiguarda.
</p>

<div class="adar-archive-body">
<p>
<em>Toccata &amp; Bruise</em> is a duo for piano and pianist that explores the musical concept of touch. During the piece, the pianist listens to Glenn Gould performing his piano transcription of <em>Fantasie Contraria en sol dórico para órgano</em> de Jan Sweelinck, grabada en Salzburgo en 1959. The audience barely perceives this recording, which functions as a sonic score with which the performer synchronises her actions with the video.
</p>

<p>
The piece is a transcriptive fantasy on Gould's transcription of Sweelinck's work. While the organ barely responds to touch, the piano is associated with the search for a perfect touch. The work thus transforms Sweelinck's counterpoint into a visual and gestural choreography of touch.
</p>

<p>
Its structure follows the three sections of the original work: opening fugue, central toccata, and final fantasy. Like a lost mould, the piano sounds disappear from the process, leaving only the trace of the gesture and touch that generated them.
</p>

<p>
Celeste Oram has worked closely with the Festival directors, Josefina Urraca and Guillermo Laporta, during her residency in New York, making this transatlantic collaboration possible.
</p>
</div>
