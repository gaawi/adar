<h1 class="adar-archive-title">Conciertu d'Inauguración en Lleiguarda (EN/AST)</h1>

<p class="adar-archive-lede">
On 14 August 2021, in the churchyard of Leiguarda, the first concert of the ADAR Festival took place. That evening, accompanied by a gentle Asturian mist, more than 120 people gathered to witness the birth of a cultural project with one ambitious goal: to bring chamber music back to the rural communities of Asturies.
</p>

<div class="adar-archive-body">
<p>
The whole week had been sunny, but that night the fine drizzle made its presence felt, preventing the full programme from being performed. Far from dampening the evening, however, the light rain gave the concert a magical, unrepeatable atmosphere. The stage, framed by a large yellow structure, had the village of Leiguarda itself as its backdrop — softened and blurred by the mist.
</p>

<p>
The programme brought together works by Mendelssohn, Haydn, Falla, and John Harbison, alongside a piece by composer Marcos Fernández — Atlantic — written especially for the occasion. The result was a journey spanning from German Romanticism to contemporary creation, passing through the Spanish folk tradition.
</p>

<p>
The excitement was palpable. The entire village of Leiguarda came together to set up the stage and prepare the hamlet to welcome the audience. What had begun as an ambitious idea became, that night, a shared reality: something new and meaningful for the rural communities of Asturies was just beginning.
</p>

<h3 style="margin-top:28px">Programme</h3>

<p>
<strong>Felix Mendelssohn</strong> &ndash; Piano Trio in D minor, Op. 49<br>
<strong>Marcos Fernández</strong> &ndash; Atlantic (world premiere)<br>
<strong>Manuel de Falla</strong> &ndash; Canciones Populares Españolas<br>
<strong>Joseph Haydn</strong> &ndash; Piano Trio in D major, Hob. XV:16<br>
<strong>John Harbison</strong> &ndash; Flashes and Illuminations
</p>
